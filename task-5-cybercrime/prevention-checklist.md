# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India
**Prepared by:** Sanjhi Taksali | 25BCE10053

---

## 8 Safety Tips

1. NEVER share your OTP with anyone — not even someone claiming
   to be from your bank, college, or government. No real organization
   will ever ask for your OTP over a call or message. Ever.

2. Set a UPI transaction limit in your banking app (example: ₹2,000
   per day). Even if someone gets access to your account, they cannot
   drain everything at once. This one setting can save your savings.

3. Always check the URL before entering your login details on any
   website. Look for https:// and verify the domain carefully.
   Fake sites use URLs like "sbi-verify.com" instead of "sbi.co.in".

4. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on your Gmail, social
   media, and banking apps. Even if someone steals your password,
   they still cannot log in without your phone.

5. Never click on links received via WhatsApp, SMS, or email that
   ask you to log in urgently, claim a prize, or verify your account.
   When in doubt, open the official app directly instead.

6. Use different passwords for different accounts. If one account
   gets hacked, the others stay safe. Use a pattern you can remember —
   for example, combine a word + platform name + a number.

7. Never do net banking or UPI payments on public or free Wi-Fi
   (like in cafes, buses, or airports). These networks can be
   monitored by attackers sitting nearby.

8. Check your bank statements and UPI transaction history at least
   once every week. Fraudulent transactions are much easier to
   reverse if you report them within the first few hours.

## If You Are a Victim — Act Immediately

- National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
- Helpline Number: 1930 (available 24x7, free to call)
- Call 1930 within 1 hour of losing money — the faster you
  report, the higher the chance of recovering your money.
- You can report: UPI fraud, phishing, cyberbullying,
  identity theft, and all online scams.
