# Cybercrime Case Study – Phishing

## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where an attacker pretends to be a
trusted organization — such as a bank, college, or government agency —
to trick victims into revealing sensitive information like passwords,
OTPs, or bank account details.

## How It Happens — Step by Step

1. The attacker creates a fake website that looks exactly like a real
   one — for example, a fake SBI net banking page or a fake IRCTC
   login page. Even the logo and colors look identical.

2. They send a mass message via email, WhatsApp, or SMS saying
   something urgent like: "Your account will be blocked in 24 hours.
   Click here immediately to verify your details."

3. The victim panics after reading the urgent message and clicks
   the link without checking if it is genuine.

4. The link opens the fake website. The victim enters their username,
   password, and OTP thinking it is the real site.

5. The attacker captures these credentials in real time and immediately
   logs into the victim's actual bank account — often transferring
   money within minutes before the victim even realizes.

6. By the time the victim notices and calls the bank, the money is
   already gone and very difficult to recover.

## Who is Usually Targeted?

Phishing attacks commonly target students, elderly people, and
first-time internet banking users who may not be familiar with how
official organizations communicate. College students are especially
targeted because they frequently use UPI apps like PhonePe, Google
Pay, and Paytm for daily transactions.

## Consequences

- Victims can lose their entire bank balance within minutes
- Personal data and identity can be sold on the dark web
- Recovering money lost to phishing in India is extremely difficult
  and can take months of legal proceedings
- Emotional stress, loss of trust, and embarrassment are common
  side effects reported by victims

**Source:** Based on publicly reported cases documented at
cybercrime.gov.in and published advisories by CERT-In
(Indian Computer Emergency Response Team, cert-in.org.in)
