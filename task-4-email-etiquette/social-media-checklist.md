# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## DO's ✅

1. Keep your profiles professional — use a decent profile photo and a clean bio.
   Employers and internship coordinators regularly search applicants online.

2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all your accounts — Gmail,
   Instagram, LinkedIn. This prevents hacking even if someone knows your password.

3. Think before you post — ask yourself: "Would I be okay if my professor
   or future employer saw this?" Anything public can be screenshotted forever.

4. Use strong, unique passwords for each platform — a mix of letters,
   numbers and symbols. Never reuse the same password across accounts.

5. Report cyberbullying or harassment immediately using the platform's
   report tool and at cybercrime.gov.in if serious.

## DON'Ts ❌

1. Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or password with anyone — not even
   someone claiming to be from your bank, college, or government.
   This is the #1 method of financial fraud in India.

2. Never post your real-time location, home address, or phone number
   publicly. This makes you vulnerable to stalking or physical harm.

3. Never click on links sent via DM, WhatsApp, or SMS from unknown
   accounts — even if they offer prizes, jobs, or say your account
   is at risk. These are almost always phishing attempts.

4. Never share photos or videos of others without their consent.
   This is punishable under the Indian IT Act.

5. Never post angry, offensive, or inappropriate comments — even if
   you delete them later. Screenshots exist and reputations are hard to rebuild.
